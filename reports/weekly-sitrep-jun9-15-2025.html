Executive Summary

The week of 9–15 June 2025 recorded 107 security incidents across Somalia resulting in 124 confirmed fatalities. Kinetic activity was dominated by battles (46 incidents, 43% of the total), with significant concurrent activity in strategic developments (34 incidents) reflecting ongoing intelligence operations, defections, and resource movements.

Three fronts drove the majority of lethal violence: Lower Shabelle (Afgooye corridor), Hiraan (Bulo Burto axis), and Bari (Bossaso/Galgala highlands). Al-Shabaab remained the most active actor (45 incidents), but government forces — particularly Danab, the SNA, and the Puntland Dervish Force — drove the week's highest single-incident fatality counts through offensive operations.

Notably, the week's deadliest incident involved Puntland Dervish Force vs. Islamic State Somalia Province (ISSP) near Bossaso — a reminder that the IS threat in northeastern Somalia remains kinetically active alongside the more prominent Al-Shabaab campaign.

Assessment
Afgooye Corridor: Multi-day Danab operations in Gaalware/Saabid signal a deliberate push to contest Al-Shabaab's hold on the corridor connecting Middle and Lower Shabelle. This route is critical for AS logistics and tax extraction — sustained pressure here has strategic significance beyond the immediate battle counts.
ISSP in Bari: The Bossaso incident (15 killed) is the deadliest IS-attributed engagement in recent weeks. Puntland Dervish Force's operational tempo against ISSP in the Galgala highlands has intensified — but the IS presence in northeast Somalia remains understated in most coverage focused on AS.
Protest activity: Five protest incidents recorded this week (Mogadishu/Benadir-focused) are consistent with ongoing political tension over government resource allocation and FMS relations. Worth monitoring for escalation into political violence.
