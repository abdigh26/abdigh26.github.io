Executive Summary

The April–June 2025 period recorded 901 security incidents and 1,940 fatalities across Somalia — a 53% increase in incidents and 50% increase in fatalities compared to the same period in 2024 (587 incidents, 1,292 fatalities). This represents one of the most violent quarters in the dataset's recent history.

Al-Shabaab remained the dominant actor (366 incidents, 41% of total), while the Somali National Army (138 incidents) and the Puntland Dervish Force (40 incidents) together accounted for nearly 20% of all recorded activity — reflecting the dual-front character of Somalia's security environment: the AS insurgency in the south-central regions, and the ISSP campaign in the northeast.

Geographically, Hirshabelle (87 incidents) and Southwest (84 incidents) bore the heaviest burden, consistent with Al-Shabaab's operational concentration in the Shabelle and Jubba river valleys. A significant new variable in this period: the recorded activity of US Military Forces (2025-) — 22 incidents — marking the formal coding of resumed American unilateral strike operations in Somalia.

Incident Trend
Monthly Incidents & Fatalities — Apr–Jun 2025 vs 2024

The 53% year-on-year surge in incidents reflects a combination of intensified government offensive operations (particularly in Lower Shabelle and Hiraan), Al-Shabaab's tactical adaptation through insurgency activities and IED use, and the entry of US forces as a recorded actor. The fatality rate per incident (2.15 in 2025 vs 2.20 in 2024) remained stable — suggesting the volume increase, not lethality per engagement, is the primary driver.

US Military Forces (2025-): The formal coding of 22 US military incidents in this period marks a significant data development. Following the resumption of unilateral American counterterrorism operations in early 2025, ACLED began coding US forces as a distinct actor. This aligns with reporting of intensified Danab-US joint operations and independent US airstrikes targeting AS command nodes — particularly in Hiraan and Lower Shabelle.
Year-on-Year Comparison
Apr–Jun 2024 vs Apr–Jun 2025
Key finding: The 53% year-on-year increase in incidents is the largest quarterly surge recorded in the 2022–2025 period. The increase is broad-based — visible across all FMS — rather than concentrated in a single theater, suggesting a structural escalation rather than a localized flare-up.
Assessment & Outlook
Structural escalation: The volume increase across all FMS — not just traditional hotspots — suggests Al-Shabaab is successfully maintaining pressure on multiple fronts simultaneously, forcing the SNA to distribute limited resources across a wide theater. This is consistent with a classic insurgent exhaustion strategy.
US re-engagement: The resumption of US unilateral operations introduces a high-capability variable that could meaningfully degrade AS command structures — but also risks civilian harm incidents that complicate the government's legitimacy narrative. The 29 "Unidentified Military Forces" incidents likely contain additional US activity not yet formally attributed.
Northeast divergence: Puntland's IS-focused campaign (40 Dervish Force incidents) represents a genuinely separate conflict dynamic from the AS-dominated south-central theater. The two fronts draw on different force sets, operate under different political frameworks, and have limited operational overlap — but both consume Somali security bandwidth.
Q3 2025 forecast: Based on the trajectory of the past three quarters, Q3 2025 (July–September) is likely to sustain elevated incident levels, with continued concentration in Hirshabelle and Southwest. Seasonal factors (reduced movement during harvest and rains) may modestly suppress AS activity in rural Lower Shabelle, but urban IED activity in Mogadishu typically persists through these periods.
Data Note

This report draws from the Somali OSINT Brief master dataset (ACLED-derived), covering 51,248+ incidents from 1997 to present, updated weekly. The reporting period (April–June 2025) reflects available ACLED data as of June 2026 — some incidents from June 2025 may be subject to revision as ACLED's verification process continues. Year-on-year comparisons use the equivalent calendar period from 2024. The full dataset is explorable at the Somali OSINT Brief Dashboard.
