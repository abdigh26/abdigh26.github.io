Platform Architecture

Three integrated intelligence layers.

Each pillar operates independently and feeds into the others — incident data informs library context, library context informs AI analysis.

I
Security Dashboard
Somalia Conflict Incident Database, 1997–Present
51,000+ verified incidents sourced from ACLED and cross-referenced field reports. Filter by actor, event type, Federal Member State, district, and date range. Updated weekly via automated pipeline. Covers battles, IED events, targeted killings, civilian violence, and political developments across all Somali administrative regions.
II
HOA Intelligence Library
Live News, Reports & Academic Literature — Daily Ingestion
Automated daily aggregation of live press, think-tank briefs, UN reports, and peer-reviewed literature covering Somalia, Ethiopia, Eritrea, Djibouti, Kenya, and the Red Sea corridor. Every entry is tagged by region, topic, and source tier. AI-powered search returns direct intelligence assessments — not links.
III
BLUF Engine
On-Demand AI Summaries — Bottom Line Up Front
Every news item carries a one-click BLUF: two sentences stating exactly what happened and the immediate tactical, political, or commercial risk impact. Generated on demand by Gemini, cached permanently. Designed for analysts who need rapid situational awareness without reading full articles.

Data Integrity

Methodology & provenance.

Institutional buyers require verifiable data chains. Every record in the platform carries traceable sourcing and follows documented classification rules.

Primary Source ACLED (Armed Conflict Location & Event Data Project) — cross-referenced with local Somali press, UN Security Council reports, and field correspondents.
Deduplication Automated MD5 fingerprint matching on Date + District + Actor + Event Type prevents duplicate records across manual daily entries and weekly automated ingestion.
Update Cycle Weekly automated sync via GitHub Actions pipeline. Daily library ingestion from RSS feeds, policy repositories, and live news sources.
Classification Events classified per ACLED schema: Battles, Explosions/Remote Violence, Violence Against Civilians, Protests, Riots, and Strategic Developments.
Coverage Span February 1997 to present. 51,200+ incident records across all Somali Federal Member States, districts, and localities.
