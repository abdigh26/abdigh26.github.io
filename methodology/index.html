Somali OSINT Brief

Methodology

How we source, verify, and rate confidence in our Horn of Africa security analysis — so you know exactly what you're reading before you act on it.

Sources

Somali OSINT Brief monitors 20 sources across Somali-language outlets (Garowe Online, Hiiraan Online, Goobjoog News, Radio Dalsan), regional Ethiopian and Red Sea coverage, and international wire and conflict-monitoring services. Incident data is cross-referenced against UCDP's conflict event database and GDELT's global event dataset.

Process

  1. Collection — RSS ingestion pulls new reporting continuously across all monitored sources.
  2. Extraction — AI-assisted analysis identifies relevant incidents, actors, and locations, and generates initial summaries (BLUF).
  3. Cross-referencing — Incidents are checked against UCDP and GDELT records where available to corroborate reporting and reduce single-source reliance.
  4. Human review — All AI-generated analysis is reviewed before publication.

Confidence levels

Verified

Corroborated by two or more independent sources, or confirmed in structured conflict datasets (UCDP/GDELT).

Reported

Sourced from a single credible outlet, not yet independently corroborated.

Assessed

Analytical judgment based on patterns, context, or incomplete reporting, clearly flagged as interpretation rather than fact.

Limitations

Open-source reporting on Somalia is uneven — some regions and incident types are underreported relative to others, particularly in areas with limited press access. We flag this where it materially affects confidence in a given assessment.
