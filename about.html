About

Somali OSINT Brief

Somali OSINT Brief is a Horn of Africa intelligence platform run by Abdi Haji, focused on conflict dynamics, insurgency patterns, and geopolitical developments across Somalia and the wider Horn of Africa region. The work sits at the intersection of ground-level incident data and strategic analysis.

This site publishes weekly situation reports, monthly trend analyses, and long-form analytical briefs — all grounded in a live dataset of 51,248+ verified security incidents sourced from ACLED and updated weekly via automated pipeline.

The Platform

SOB operates three integrated intelligence layers. The Security Dashboard provides interactive access to the full incident dataset — filterable by actor, event type, Federal Member State, and date range. The HOA Intelligence Library aggregates live news, think-tank reports, UN documents, and academic literature covering Somalia, Ethiopia, Eritrea, Djibouti, Kenya, and the Red Sea corridor — updated daily. The BLUF Engine generates on-demand AI summaries for every news item, delivering two-sentence intelligence assessments built for rapid situational awareness.

The Data

The underlying dataset tracks security incidents across Somalia from February 1997 to present, covering incident type, actors, locations, fatalities, and administrative geography across all Federal Member States. It is updated automatically every week from ACLED's conflict database, with deduplication logic to maintain data integrity across manual and automated entries.

The Approach

Ground-level data meets grand strategy. Every analytical claim is traceable to verified incident records. No vague assessments, no speculation presented as fact. Institutional clients — embassies, NGOs, security firms, and logistics operators — can request full platform access via the capability brief.
